# 📚 English Vocabulary - 2026-03-09

## Community

- **Definition:** A group of people living in the same place or having a particular characteristic in common.
- **Pronunciation:** /kəˈmjuːnəti/
- **Part of Speech:** Noun
- **Example Sentence:** The local community came together to clean up the park.
- **Synonyms:** Society, public, population, neighborhood.
- **Notes:** It can also refer to a group of people with shared interests or values, regardless of their location.

---
## Scandinavian

- **Definition:** A group of countries in Northern Europe, including Denmark, Norway, Sweden, Iceland, and Finland.
- **Pronunciation:** /ˌskændɪˈneɪviən/
- **Part of Speech:** Noun
- **Example Sentence:** The Scandinavian countries have a high standard of living and are known for their welfare systems.
- **Synonyms:** Nordic, Scandinavian countries, Scandinavian Peninsula, Scandinavia.
- **Notes:** While often used interchangeably with "Nordic," strictly speaking, Scandinavia refers only to Denmark, Norway, and Sweden.

---

## Context

- **Definition:** The circumstances that form the setting for an event, statement, or idea, and in terms of which it can be fully understood.
- **Pronunciation:** /ˈkɒntekst/
- **Part of Speech:** Noun
- **Example Sentence:** The context of the situation was important in understanding the full meaning of the message.
- **Synonyms:** Setting, circumstances, environment, backdrop.
- **Notes:** In linguistics, it refers to the parts of a written or spoken statement that precede or follow a specific word or passage, usually influencing its meaning or effect.

---

## ambiguous

- **Definition:** Having more than one possible meaning or interpretation.
- **Pronunciation:** /æmˈbɪɡjuəs/
- **Part of Speech:** Adjective
- **Example Sentence:** The meaning of the word was ambiguous, and it was unclear which interpretation was intended.
- **Synonyms:** Unclear, doubtful, uncertain, indeterminate.
- **Notes:** It is often used to describe language or situations that are not clearly defined.

---

## deliberately

- **Definition:** Done with care and attention to detail.
- **Pronunciation:** /dɪˈlɪbərətli/
- **Part of Speech:** Adverb
- **Example Sentence:** She deliberated for hours before making her decision.
- **Synonyms:** Carefully, deliberately, methodically, purposefully.
- **Notes:** It is often used to describe actions that are carefully planned or executed.

---

## nuance

- **Definition:** A subtle difference in meaning or interpretation.
- **Pronunciation:** /njuːəns/
- **Part of Speech:** Noun
- **Example Sentence:** The nuance of the situation was important in understanding the full meaning of the message.
- **Synonyms:** Subtle difference, nuance, nuance, nuance.
- **Notes:** Often used in the context of communication, art, and social interactions to describe subtle shades of meaning or expression.

---

## subtle

- **Definition:** (especially of a change or distinction) so delicate or precise as to be difficult to analyze or describe.
- **Pronunciation:** /ˈsʌtl/
- **Part of Speech:** Adjective
- **Example Sentence:** The subtle difference in the colors made all the difference.
- **Synonyms:** Delicate, precise, subtle, subtle.
- **Notes:** The 'b' is silent, and the word is often used to describe things that are understated or not immediately obvious.
